Fiscal Fragility & Political Tension: Kenya is grappling with a high debt burden (67.8% of GDP, elevated risk of debt distress), persistent fiscal deficits, and social unrest following tax reform attempts in 2024. Revenue lags expenditure despite a tax/GDP ratio (14.1%) that is close to the 15% minimum.

🟡

Debt Distress Risk

Public debt at 71.6% of GDP with elevated risk of distress. Debt service consumes 24.3% of government revenue — crowding out development spending.

🟢

Better Tax Base

Tax/GDP at 14.1% — close to the 15% minimum and notably stronger than regional peers. Broadening base and improving compliance remain priorities.

🟡

Missing B-Ready Data

Kenya is not yet covered by the World Bank B-Ready assessment — limiting visibility on regulatory framework, public services, and operational efficiency for investors.

🟠

Sub-Investment Grade

S&P B rating after downgrades signals high credit risk and elevated borrowing costs. Market access strained after 2024 Eurobond refinancing.
