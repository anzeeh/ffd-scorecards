⚖️

A stable but low-investment equilibrium: Guatemala pairs genuine macro stability — low inflation, public debt near 26% of GDP, a current-account surplus and reserves above 7 months of imports — with the hemisphere's lowest tax take (~12% of GDP), under-funded social services, weak rule of law, and heavy reliance on remittances (~19% of GDP) now exposed to shifts in US trade and migration policy.

🟠

The Revenue Floor

At ~12% of GDP, tax revenue is among the lowest of any market-access economy in the Western Hemisphere — the binding constraint on a "small-footprint state" with weak social outcomes.

🟡

Remittance Dependency

Remittances (~19% of GDP) dwarf FDI (~1.6%) and ODA combined. They sustain consumption but mask weak competitiveness — and are exposed to tighter US migration policy.

🔴

Social & Governance Deficit

Central-government health (~1.4%) and education (~2.9%) spending are low; secondary enrolment is below 50%. CPI sits at 25/100 and rule of law remains weak amid an executive–judiciary standoff.

🟢

Macro-Financial Strength

Public debt ~26% of GDP, banking NPLs ~2.5%, a BB+ rating one notch below investment grade, and ample reserves give Guatemala space its peers lack — if it chooses to use it.

On data gaps & scoring. Pillar and overall scores use blank-aware averaging — missing indicators are excluded, never treated as zero. Six indicators are currently un-scored for Guatemala: the three B-READY pillars (Guatemala not yet assessed), the two Enterprise Survey firm-finance indicators (last survey 2017, too dated), and the IMF Financial Development Index (Guatemala's value could not be verified against an accessible primary source — see note below). The bank capital-to-assets score (2) reflects the unweighted leverage ratio; on a risk-weighted basis the banking system is well-capitalised (CAR ~15.7%). WGI Rule-of-Law (−1.08) and Regulatory-Quality (−0.29) are the 2024 World Bank WGI point estimates on the −2.5 to +2.5 standard-normal scale — distinct from the newer 0–100 WGI score. Where the IMF Article IV (CR 25/260) is the source, cards are tagged IMF AIV 2025.

Sources & Methodology: Macro-fiscal and financial-sector indicators are drawn primarily from the IMF 2025 Article IV Consultation for Guatemala (Country Report No. 25/260, Sep 2025). Governance, integrity, AML and aid indicators use World Bank WGI, Transparency International CPI 2024, the Basel AML Index 2024, World Bank WDI, PEFA, and UN DESA. Scores (1–5) reflect assessment against fixed international benchmarks; pillar averages and the overall FfD score use blank-aware (AVERAGEIF) logic. Values marked "~" are estimates or single-source figures pending verification.

Intended Use: This scorecard is a structured conversation starter for donor–government dialogue on FfD priorities. It is not an official government document. Data should be verified against primary sources before use in formal assessments.

Critical Context: Guatemala's challenge is not instability but a low-investment, "small-footprint state" equilibrium — strong buffers and market access alongside the hemisphere's lowest revenue mobilisation, under-funded social services, weak governance, and remittance dependency now exposed to US trade and migration shifts. FfD dialogue should be framed around converting fiscal space into well-executed, high-quality public investment.
