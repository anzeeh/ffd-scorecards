Critical Transition Moment: Bangladesh graduates from LDC status in November 2026, losing preferential trade access & concessional financing eligibility — while facing a deepening revenue crisis (tax/GDP fell to 6.9% in FY25), a banking sector in acute distress (system-wide NPLs surged to 34%), and political transition uncertainty ahead of February 2026 elections. GDP growth slowed to 3.7% in FY25.

🔴

Revenue Crisis

Tax revenue fell further to 6.9% of GDP in FY25 (down from 7.4% in FY24), driven by compliance weaknesses, NBR staff strikes, and public unrest — roughly half the 15% minimum needed for basic state functions.

🟢

Remittance Lifeline

Remittances at 6.1% of GDP fall within the healthy 3–10% band — a critical flow that exceeds FDI and ODA combined.

🔴

Spending Gaps

Education (2.0% GDP) and especially health (0.31% GDP) spending are among the lowest in Asia — well below UNESCO and WHO benchmarks.

🔴

Banking System Crisis

System-wide NPLs surged to 34% at end-June 2025 following stricter asset classification. Reported capital ratio of 4.5% is overstated; 27 banks in regulatory forbearance. Five insolvent Islamic banks being nationalised and merged.
